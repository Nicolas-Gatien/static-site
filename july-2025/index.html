Here's a quick update of what I've been up to in July!

1. I'm visualizing HD brain data

In June, I managed to graph some EEG data. In July, I stepped it up and made some visualizations of neuronal activity collected from HD microelectrode arrays. Here's one of the visualizations I generated.


You can see the bursts of white pixels showing activity in the recorded neurons. Many of the grey pixels are just noise. I wrote a little more about how I made this visualization in this post: visualizing-mea-data-difference

My next steps are to perform spike sorting on the dataset to figure out what part of this is noise, and what part is actual neuron activity.

I train in a sport called HEMA (Historical European Martial Arts) and one of the disciplines I train is highland broadsword.
