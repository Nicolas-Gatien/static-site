Notebox App

2025-10-24 • Nicolas Gatien • idea

I regularly make use of my box of notecards when I'm reading new material. I find this system of taking notes and anchoring them to previous ideas works remarkably well for me. I would like to have a system similar when I am not able to access my physical notecards. Many people refer to a system like this as a Zettelkasten.

Many apps, such as Obsidian or Logseq market themselves as capable of implementing a Zettelkasten system. While I believe this to be true, it doesn't quite encompass the same feel as my physical cards.

I've been thinking of making a notecard focused app. Where the notes are on digital notecards. Here are the specifications I'm thinking of:

  1. A notecard is restrained to a maximum number of characters (about 3x the twitter character limit)
  2. A notecard must have a location in the box (determined by an address)
  3. Writing another notecard's address in the text of a notecard turns the address into a clickable hyperlink.
  4. While viewing a notecard, the two notecards preceding and proceeding it are displayed beneath and above.
  5. Notecards cannot be erased or moved once added and placed in the box
  6. Once a notecard is saved, the text on the notecard cannot be deleted, but it can be struck-through and more text can be added (if it's not yet at the character limit)
  7. It must be a downloadable app and work offline

That's it.

That is all I would implement for the first version, and then I would begin using it. Based on how I use it, I might make a few adjustments but this is all I need.
